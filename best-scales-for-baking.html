Why Weight Beats Volume in Baking

A cup of all-purpose flour measured by scooping can weigh anywhere from 120g to 165g depending on how packed it is. That 37% variance directly impacts your bread rise, cookie spread, and cake texture. A scale eliminates this entirely — 150g is always 150g.

Once you start baking by weight, you'll never go back. It's also faster: add ingredients directly to your mixing bowl, taring between additions, without dirtying a single measuring cup.
